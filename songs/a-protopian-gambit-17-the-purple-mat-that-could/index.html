The Purple Mat That Could

A Protopian Gambit - Track 17

musicstoryimagination

About this song

The Purple Mat That Could is about making practical choices during difficult times without losing joy, responsibility or hope.

Lyrics

Australia.
Not the one on travel ads.
The real one.
Where sunscreen’s a survival tool,
and the gap between rich and regular
is wider than a politician’s memory under oath.

Where climate reports get buried,
and public housing gets sold
while private jets line up like seagulls at a servo pie.

Where the best ideas
don’t come from boardrooms or Canberra pressers,
but from the backyard,
the beach,
and sometimes,
the bottom stall of a dodgy pub toilet.

Because this one?
This one started with a lavender-scented urinal mat.

Anti-splash.
Anti-corruption.
Anti “mate, just trust us.”

Two locals.
Minjerribah.
Straddie, if you're mainland.
They weren’t gunning for power.
They were sick of the smell.

Of politics. Of pass-the-blame.
Of policy made in rooms
where no one's listening,
especially not to people who can't donate.

So they made a mat.
Purple.
Soft.
Scented like irony.

Put it under the system.
Catch the spray.
Save the floor.

But under that joke?
A question.
What if we made leaders go inward,
before we let them speak outward?

What if integrity wasn't a slogan,
but a measurable, mapped, felt thing?

So they built it.
Not just a mat.
A protocol.

Sixty sessions.
Two hours each.
In a pressurised chamber
breathing pure oxygen at two atmospheres.
(More pressure than Question Time,
but less hot air.)

Inside, AI helps you unpack yourself —
memories, contradictions, old wounds,
how you vote, how you lie,
how you justify scrolling instead of calling your mum back.

And when you finish?
You get your Aura Twin —
A digital echo of who you are,
built from actual evidence,
not a campaign video.

No filters.
No spin.
No “I was misquoted.”

Just you.
Laid bare.
Backed up.

And the real kicker?
They want every future politician to do it.
No Genesis, no candidacy.

Because if you can’t sit still with your soul for 120 hours,
how the hell can you sit in office and serve 25 million people?

If you can’t see your own bias,
your shadow,
your trauma turned talking point —
why should we believe you know ours?

Meanwhile, AuraOS — the software behind it —
is already being used in aged care.
Helping people with dementia
recall songs,
laugh at old jokes,
look their twin in the face and say,
“I remember.”

It’s working.
And not because it’s magic.
But because it listens.
Because it respects the person
instead of performing respect.

Compare that
to the current political theatre
of shouting, sniping,
and saying “Australians want...”
without asking one.

This tech isn’t perfect.
But it’s honest.

And that’s already
more than we can say for the nightly news.

So yeah.
Maybe change starts with
a lavender urinal mat.
Maybe the revolution is scented.

Maybe self-reflection
smells better than spin.

So next time someone tells you
nothing ever changes,
or that politics is always like this —

Remind them.

Change doesn’t always wear a suit.
Sometimes it wears purple,
smells like eucalyptus and subtle rebellion,
and soaks up more crap
than the entire press gallery combined.

Flush the fear.
Map your mind.
Smell the shift.

And leave the stall
cleaner than you found it.
